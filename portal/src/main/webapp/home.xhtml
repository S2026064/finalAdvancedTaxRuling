What is an Advance Tax Ruling?

An Advance Tax Ruling is a written statement issued by SARS that provides clarity and certainty on the Commissioner’s interpretation and application of the tax laws regarding a proposed transaction. A taxpayer (applicant) can apply for a Binding Private or a Binding Class Ruling.

What is a Binding Private Ruling?

A Binding Private Ruling is a written statement issued by SARS regarding the application of a tax act to one or more parties to a proposed transaction.

What is a Binding Class Ruling?

A Binding Class Ruling is a written statement issued by SARS regarding the application of a tax act to a specific class of persons in respect of a proposed transaction.

A class would be shareholders, members, beneficiaries (or similar) in respect of a company, association, pension fund or trust; or a group of persons that may be unrelated but are similarly affected by the proposed transaction and agree to be represented by the applicant.

Are there any fees payable?

Yes, in terms of section 81(1) of the Tax Administration Act the Commissioner may prescribe an application and a cost recovery fee to defray the cost of the ‘advance ruling’ system. There are three payments required during the ruling application process:

  1. Application fee – a non-refundable fee that does not form part of the total cost of the application.
  2. Deposit (advance payment) on the cost recovery fee – calculated at 20% of the higher range of the estimated fee range.
  3. Final payment of the outstanding balance in respect of the cost recovery fee – to settle the invoice for the work performed/time spent in processing the ruling request.
